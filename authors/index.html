Authors

Brennan Barnard
Director of College Counseling, The Derryfield School

I am the director of college counseling and outreach at The Derryfield School in New Hampshire, an independent college preparatory school grades 6-12. In addition, I serve as the director of college counseling at US Performance Academy, an online high school for competitive athletes. For two decades I have worked as teacher, dorm parent, advisor, coach, admission officer and student affairs administrator in independent high schools and colleges. I serve on the advisory board for the New Hampshire College and University Council’s New Hampshire Scholars Program. I also serve on the executive committee for the Character Collaborative. I have written about college admission for the New York Times, Washington Post, HuffPost, Concord Monitor and Journal of College Admission. A practicing Quaker, I am the father of two and live in Hopkinton, New Hampshire where I am a volunteer firefighter. Follow my Forbes writing: https://www.forbes.com/sites/brennanbarnard/#35990421783f

Rick Clark
Director of Undergraduate Admission, Georgia Tech

I am currently the Director of Undergraduate Admission at Georgia Tech where I have been on staff for sixteen year. Prior to joining Tech, I worked at Wake Forest University, Georgia State University, and The McCallie School. My admission experience also includes serving on a number of regional and national committees, including the SACAC Governing Board, NACAC Government Relations Committee, ACT Council, and the College Board Search Advisory Board. In addition to my work on campus and around the country, I travel annually to U.S. embassies and consulates with the Department of State's Office of Overseas Schools. My background also includes serving as Tech's first elected Chairman of Staff Council and being a member of the 2018 Georgia Leadership Class- and organization which exists to cultivate, educate, and connect rising leaders. I live in Avondale Estates, GA with my wife and two kids.
